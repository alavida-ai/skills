Methodology Notes

Chart type selection follows the Cleveland-McGill hierarchy from the methodology playbook:

The scatter plot (Observable Plot) encodes effort and impact as position on two axes — the most accurate encoding for the "Correlate" reader task. Category color is limited to 3 values (under the 5-color memory limit).

The radar chart (Chart.js) profiles the team across 6 dimensions. Two overlaid datasets (current vs target) use similarity (same shape) and color difference to invite comparison. Radar is appropriate here because the reader task is "compare profiles", not "compare precise values".

The treemap (D3.js) shows part-of-whole distribution via containment and area — appropriate when the reader needs to see how a total decomposes into categories. Nested hierarchy (Features → Auth, Dashboard, API) adds a second level of detail.

Annotation layer: The scatter plot includes an editorial callout highlighting the quick-win cluster. Each data-heavy section has a descriptive paragraph serving as contextual annotation.
